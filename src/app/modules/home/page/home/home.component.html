OUR
Products
Pyrolysis oil, derived from plastic waste, is a versatile and sustainable fuel with extensive industrial applications. Produced through an advanced thermal decomposition process, it serves as an eco-friendly alternative to traditional fossil fuels like diesel and furnace oil. Its high calorific value and combustion efficiency make it ideal for powering industrial boilers, turbines, and heavy-duty machinery. Additionally, pyrolysis oil is a valuable raw material in the chemical and petrochemical industries, where it can be refined into various useful products such as solvents and synthetic gases.
Pyrolysis oil
Waste plastic derived from pyrolysis of waste plastic trash and municipal waste is one of the promising alternative fuels because of its similar carbon chain characteristics and physical properties to diesel fuel. Pyrolysis oil is a complex mixture of hydrocarbons, similar to crude oil. pyrolysis oil can be used as a alternative for conventional fossil fuels in various applications, including power generation and heating. The conversion of plastic waste into oil can help reduce the amount of plastic ending up in landfilling and Incineration. waste management solution of emissions and residues is essential to minimise environmental impact and ensure the sustainability of pyrolysis oil production.
Black Carbon
Carbon black, a fine black powder mostly composed of elemental carbon, is produced when hydrocarbons are not completely burned. By acting as a common reinforcing filler, carbon black greatly improves the mechanical qualities of rubber goods, such as tensile strength, abrasion resistance, and tear resistance. It is also essential to several sectors, including printing, electronics, building, and the automobile sector. It is a useful pigment in paints, inks, and polymers because of its remarkable tinting and UV-stabilizing qualities. Carbon black may also be used to make battery electrodes, as a black pigment in printing toners, and as a conductive component in electronic products.
Efurnace Oil
Furnace oil also known as fuel or heating oil—is an essential part of the energy landscape, particularly for heating applications in various industrial sectors. furnace oil, which is produced by distilling crude oil after it has been refined, is a flexible and effective fuel source. it’s another pivotal characteristic is its viscosity, which determines its flow properties and suitability for different heating systems this oil is classified into various grades, ranging from light to heavy, based on viscosity and sulphur content. The specific grade used depends on the requirements of the heating equipment and regulatory standards. This classification underscores the importance of tailoring furnace oil to meet particular heating needs while adhering to established industry and environmental guidelines.
